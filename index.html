Violet M. Kimble is a neuroscientist and STEM advocate. She is an alumna of Drew University, where she earned her BS in neuroscience with a minor in statistics.

Currently, Violet is pursuing a Ph.D. in neuroscience at Yale University. Her research interest is applying bioinformatics tools to understand complex brain disorders and diseases.

Outside of academia, Violet works to amplify the voice of underrepresented groups of scientists. She volunteers as the Lead Membership Coordinator for The Black Women in Computational Biology Network (BWCB). With now 160+ worldwide members, the BWCB team works to ensure everyone feels welcome, affirmed, and supported as a Black womxn computational biologist.
